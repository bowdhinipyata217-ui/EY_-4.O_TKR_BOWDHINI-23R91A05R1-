A "Ferrari wheel" is a metaphorical or potentially misspelled term, as it seems to be a confusion between a Ferris wheel, a large rotating observation wheel, and the luxury car brand Ferrari. Ferris wheels are mechanical structures with a rotating framework, typically supporting passenger cabins (gondolas), that provide panoramic views. They originated from George Washington Gale Ferris Jr.'s invention for the 1893 Chicago World's Fair and are now found in various locations worldwide, including amusement parks, carnivals, and major cities, with some of the largest located in places like Dubai, Orlando, and various Asian cities.
